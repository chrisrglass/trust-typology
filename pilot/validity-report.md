## Multi-Option Items: G2 and G3 (High Signal)

### G2: International Enrollment Stance
The four options reveal four distinct logics that don't collapse left-right:

| Option | Meaning | Who chooses it |
|---|---|---|
| A | Pro-international, full | LL (36%) |
| B | Revenue-capture critique (left skepticism) | LOL (82%), TOM (33%) |
| C | Domestic-first restriction | NAR (100%), UR (100%) |
| D | Conditional (access-protective) | LP (83%), OOL (100%), PPR (82%) |

**Finding:** G2 is the clearest cross-cutting item in the instrument. The LP and LOL split (D vs. B) and the LL and NAR split (A vs. C) represent theoretically meaningful fault lines that would not appear in a simple liberal-conservative scale.

## Structural Observation: Loyal Liberals as a Distinct Class

LL is the most internally coherent group and the clearest anchor against which to compare. They:
- Choose B (skeptical/negative) on 0-36% of items where all other groups choose 70-100%
- Are the only group choosing A on R-B (reform is sufficient) and R-A1 (self-reform possible)
- Are the only group choosing A on G1 (global mission)
- Trust administrators more than any other group (FA-A-administrators mean: 2.73 vs. NAR: 4.00)

This creates a natural "trust anchor" at one pole of the latent class structure.
